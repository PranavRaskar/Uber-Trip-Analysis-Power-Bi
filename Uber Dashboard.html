üß≠ Overview Insights

1. Overall Trip and Booking Overview

Uber recorded 103.7K bookings worth $1.6M, with an average fare of $15. Trips averaged 3 miles and 16 minutes, showing Uber's focus on short urban rides.

2. Total Booking by Payment Type

Uber Pay leads with 67% of bookings, followed by Cash (32%). Digital payment use dominates, though a small cash segment still exists in some regions.

3. Total Booking by Trip Type (Day vs. Night)

Day trips = 70%, Night trips = 30%. Uber mainly serves daytime commuters, with steady but lower night demand.

4. Total Booking Amount by Payment Type

Uber Pay generates $1.1M (71%), Cash $443K (29%). Digital users ride more and spend more per trip.

5. Total Booking Amount by Trip Type

Day trips = $1.06M (69%), Night = $490K (31%). Peak activity occurs during the day due to work-related travel.

6. Total Trip Distance by Payment Type

Uber Pay covers 231K miles (66%), Cash 115K miles (33%). Digital users tend to travel longer distances.

7. Total Trip Distance by Trip Type

Day = 230K miles (66%), Night = 119K miles (34%). Confirms Uber's daytime dominance in both bookings and distance.

8. Conclusion

Uber's operations are daytime-focused, digitally driven, and optimized for short city rides. Uber Pay powers most revenue and distance, while cash users remain a target for digital growth. Expanding night rides and eco-options like Uber Green could boost future performance.
